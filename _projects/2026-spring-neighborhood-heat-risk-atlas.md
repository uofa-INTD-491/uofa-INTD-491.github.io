## Problem

City heat reports were technically detailed but difficult to use in neighborhood-level conversations. The team focused on making local heat vulnerability easier to explain in planning workshops and community meetings.

## Data

The atlas combines land-surface temperature rasters, tree canopy estimates, cooling-center locations, transit access, and census indicators that help frame exposure and response capacity.

## Method

The team designed a small set of layered views rather than a large analytical dashboard. Each view answers one question clearly: where heat is highest, where shade is lowest, and where residents have fewer nearby cooling resources.

## Results

Workshop participants could compare neighborhoods faster and explain the trade-offs between exposure and available support without needing to interpret a full GIS workflow.

## How to run

1. Clone `https://github.com/example/neighborhood-heat-risk-atlas`.
2. Install the project dependencies listed in that repository.
3. Start the local web server and open the atlas in a browser.
