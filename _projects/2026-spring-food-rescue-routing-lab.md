## Problem

Volunteer-led food rescue runs were being coordinated through spreadsheets and message threads, which made it hard to compare route options or respond quickly when pickup timing changed.

## Data

The routing lab uses donor pickup windows, estimated travel times, refrigeration notes, delivery priorities, and volunteer availability collected from a semester pilot.

## Method

The team built a constrained routing prototype tuned for small community operations rather than large fleet optimization. It emphasizes transparent trade-offs so volunteers can adjust the plan without specialist tools.

## Results

Students could compare feasible route plans much faster and explain why one route protected delivery timing better than another when handoffs changed late in the day.

## How to run

1. Clone `https://github.com/example/food-rescue-routing-lab`.
2. Install the project dependencies and load the provided sample routing data.
3. Launch the local planner and open the app in a browser.
